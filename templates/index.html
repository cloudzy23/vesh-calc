How examples look

  • sin(x) — standard sine curve.
  • x^2 - 4 — parabola; roots at -2 and 2 are shown.
  • y=cos(x) — same as cos(x).
  • x=cos(t), y=sin(t) — unit circle (parametric).
  • x^2+y^2=1 — implicit circle using contour.
  • z = x^2 + y^2 — 3D surface (bowl).
  • r = 2*cos(t) — polar cardioid/limacon style curve.
  • x^2+y^2 <= 4 — shaded disk region (inequality).
